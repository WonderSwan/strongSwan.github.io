F.A.Q.

General

  • What is strongSwan.net?

    strongSwan.net is a VPN tunneling service which enables you to use a secure and anonymous connection to the internet.

  • What protocols does strongSwan.net support?

    We support IKEv2.

  • Can I use your VPN instead of my current ISP connection?

    No, you need existing (Cable, DSL, dial up, Satellite, etc) internet connection to be able to use VPN service.

  • Do you have any bandwidth limitation?

    There is no bandwidth limitation set on our VPN users. Our servers are connected to multiple 10Gbit/s backbone providing ultra high speed connections around the globe.

  • What Operating systems and Devices does strongSwan.net support?

    strongSwan.net works on all Windows and Unix/Linux systems, iPhone, iPad, Android and Blackberry devices.

  • Do you keep logs?

    We don't keep any logs of any kind. All we ask from you is your email address. No other information is kept on our servers.

  • Who “owns” my data?

    You own your data. No one else. You can also read our privacy policy and terms of use for more information

  • Do you keep ANY logs which would allow you or a 3rd party to match an IP-address and a time stamp to a user of your service?

    We don't store any kind of data logs on our servers. There is even no time stamp or logs of which IP-address you got assigned.



Troubleshooting

  • I am receiving Error Code 809, What should I do?

    Trying to connect again later.
    Here are some real common errors and Microsoft's explanation for them:

    Error 809 reads: The network connection between your computer and the VPN server could not be established because the remote server is not responding. This could be because one of the network devices (e.g., firewalls, NAT, routers, etc) between your computer and the remote server is not configured to allow VPN connections. Please contact your Administrator or your service provider to determine which device may be causing the problem.

  • + I am receiving an authentication failure error. How do I connect?

    Please make sure you are entering the username and password correctly. If you have lost your password, please contact us to RESET PASSWORD. You will only receive authentication failure when there is a real authentication failure. This is not a connection issue.

  • I lost my password. What should I do?

    If you have lost your password, please contact us to RESET PASSWORD with your payment email address.

  • I'm not having the best speed while connected to the VPN, is there any advice you can give me on improving speeds?

    1.strongSwan.net does not throttle any servers.
    2.strongSwan.net constantly checks to make sure servers are not overloaded.
    3.strongSwan.net will not overload our servers with accounts. All servers have set number of accounts, and when they have been used, that server becomes unavailable.

    Advice
    Make sure you are connected to the closest location possible for your desired country.
    Make sure you have tried all available networks for your closest city.

    If you do suffer from slow speed, please consider that the following factors will also effect your VPN speed.
    1.The speed of your computer, if it's overloaded everything is slower.
    2.The speed of your own Internet connection.
    3.The speed of your ISP routes to the vpn server.

  • Can I increase the speed of my connection when using VPN service?

    Sometimes users do report the VPN bypasses their ISP's throttles. Some users do report applications run better as well such as P2P.

  • I can connect to your VPN, but can't see a particular website?

    1.DNS - It's possible if you are in a country such as China, they have hijacked DNS settings for particular sites such as facebook.
       As a remedy we suggest changing your DNS server settings to googles (8.8.8.8 ; 8.8.4.4), but there are better choices:
       Primary: 85.214.20.141 Digitalcourage
       Secondary: 77.109.148.136 / 77.109.148.137 Swiss Privacy Foundation
    2.Browser Cache - Your Cache may need to be cleared as your old information from when you try to connected before is in there.
       You could also connect from a browser you have yet to connect to the site from.
    3.Time Zone - Try changing your time zone to the time zone in the area of the website you want to connect to.
    4.Reboot - If all else fails, reboot and try.

  • I can connect, but cannot browse?

    Try to connect again.

  • Why should I want to switch to a different server?

    1.The Internet is not perfect - sometimes switching to a different location will increase speed.
       The problem may be a temporarily one and a selection is very nice to have.
    2.You have moved - Generally you want to connect to a VPN server that is closet to you.
    3.IP Change - Sometimes customers want to appear on a different IP. With a simple server switch, this will accomplish that.
